## Sector Adapter: Real Estate

- Sector definition: REITs, property owners, developers, brokers, property managers, real estate service firms, and real estate software providers.
- Main business models: Rental income, leasing spreads, occupancy-driven NOI, development profits, brokerage commissions, property management fees, software subscriptions.
- Value chain notes: Site selection, underwriting, development, leasing, tenant service, maintenance, financing, brokerage, and asset management create multiple information-heavy workflows.
- Key revenue drivers: Occupancy, rental rates, leasing velocity, same-store NOI, transaction volumes, property values, development pipelines, tenant retention.
- Key cost centers: Property operations labor, maintenance, utilities pass-through management, leasing expense, G&A, financing costs, construction and permitting for developers.
- High-probability Gen AI use cases: Leasing support, tenant communication, underwriting memo drafting, property management automation, maintenance dispatch support, broker workflow tools, market research synthesis.
- Adoption bottlenecks: Fragmented ownership, inconsistent data quality, local-market complexity, human relationship intensity in leasing and brokerage, integration with legacy property systems.
- Likely beneficiaries: Property software vendors, scaled managers lowering overhead, brokers improving productivity, owners with large portfolios that can spread digital investment across many assets.
- Incumbents at risk: Labor-heavy service layers, smaller fragmented operators with weak systems, brokerage tasks exposed to workflow automation, admin-intensive property managers.
- Important KPIs or signals investors should watch: G&A as a percent of revenue, leasing cycle time, tenant retention, occupancy, maintenance response time, broker productivity, NOI margin, same-store expense growth.
- Sector-specific analytical traps to avoid: Do not claim Gen AI overrides interest-rate and supply-demand fundamentals; do not overstate near-term rent growth effects; do not ignore the fragmented, local, relationship-driven nature of real estate operations.
