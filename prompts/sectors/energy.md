## Sector Adapter: Energy

- Sector definition: Upstream oil and gas, midstream, downstream and refining, oilfield services, power trading, and energy technology providers.
- Main business models: Commodity production, volume-based transport, refining spreads, service contracts, trading and optimization, equipment sales and maintenance.
- Value chain notes: Exploration, drilling, production optimization, maintenance, trading, scheduling, field operations, compliance, and customer support vary widely by subsector.
- Key revenue drivers: Commodity prices, production volumes, utilization rates, spread capture, drilling activity, contract awards, reserve replacement, customer throughput.
- Key cost centers: Field labor, maintenance, energy input costs, transportation, safety and compliance, exploration expense, downtime, procurement, emissions monitoring.
- High-probability Gen AI use cases: Technical document search, maintenance planning support, field service knowledge retrieval, trader research workflow acceleration, permitting documentation, engineering drafting, customer and supplier communications.
- Adoption bottlenecks: Safety-critical operations, fragmented legacy systems, uneven data quality from field assets, cybersecurity concerns, workforce skepticism, limited tolerance for model error in control environments.
- Likely beneficiaries: Service and software providers that improve maintenance and engineering productivity, firms with large technical document sets, operators able to automate back-office and commercial workflows.
- Incumbents at risk: Knowledge-work-heavy service layers without proprietary data, admin-heavy operators, smaller players unable to spread digital investment across a large asset base.
- Important KPIs or signals investors should watch: LOE per barrel, downtime, maintenance backlog, drilling cycle time, SG&A per asset, trader productivity, safety incident trends, capex efficiency, field crew utilization.
- Sector-specific analytical traps to avoid: Do not present Gen AI as a primary driver of commodity outcomes; do not blur Gen AI with broader industrial automation; do not assume material production uplift without evidence from operational deployment.
