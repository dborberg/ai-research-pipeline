## Sector Adapter: Consumer Discretionary

- Sector definition: Retail, e-commerce, restaurants, travel, apparel, autos, leisure, consumer services, and discretionary brands.
- Main business models: Product margin, same-store sales, direct-to-consumer sales, franchising, advertising or marketplace revenue, ticketing, financing, aftermarket service.
- Value chain notes: Merchandising, marketing, pricing, inventory planning, digital commerce, store labor, fulfillment, personalization, and customer service are core pressure points.
- Key revenue drivers: Traffic, conversion, average ticket, pricing, category mix, occupancy, financing availability, consumer confidence, ad efficiency, repeat purchase.
- Key cost centers: Labor, fulfillment, marketing, rent, returns, inventory markdowns, customer support, content creation, dealer incentives.
- High-probability Gen AI use cases: Personalized merchandising, marketing creative generation, customer service, search and discovery, demand planning support, product content creation, store associate assist, travel itinerary and upsell tools.
- Adoption bottlenecks: Brand risk, data fragmentation across channels, uneven consumer trust, integration with merchandising systems, franchise complexity, seasonal demand volatility.
- Likely beneficiaries: Scaled digital retailers, travel platforms, brands with rich first-party data, marketplace operators, software vendors improving marketing and service productivity.
- Incumbents at risk: Retailers reliant on inefficient manual merchandising, intermediaries with weak customer ownership, brands with poor data capture, service models exposed to automated discovery and support.
- Important KPIs or signals investors should watch: Conversion, CAC, repeat rate, markdown rate, return rate, digital mix, labor hours per transaction, ad ROI, basket size, occupancy or booking conversion.
- Sector-specific analytical traps to avoid: Do not assume personalization automatically expands demand; do not ignore that many gains may be competed away into lower prices or higher marketing intensity; do not treat flashy consumer-facing AI as proof of margin improvement.
