## Sector Adapter: Financials

- Sector definition: Banks, insurers, asset managers, brokers, exchanges, payments firms, credit platforms, and financial infrastructure providers.
- Main business models: Net interest income, fee income, spread capture, underwriting, float income, advisory fees, premiums, AUM-based fees, transaction fees, data and market infrastructure revenue.
- Value chain notes: Customer acquisition, onboarding, risk assessment, underwriting, servicing, compliance, product cross-sell, claims or collections, and balance-sheet or platform monetization.
- Key revenue drivers: Loan growth, deposit mix, spread environment, premium growth, claims ratio, AUM flows, trading volumes, payment volume, customer lifetime value, retention.
- Key cost centers: Compliance and controls, operations labor, servicing, technology spend, fraud losses, customer support, distribution expense, claims handling.
- High-probability Gen AI use cases: Advisor copilots, document review, compliance workflow acceleration, customer service, underwriting memo drafting, fraud investigation support, claims intake, collections scripting, internal knowledge retrieval.
- Adoption bottlenecks: Model governance, explainability, conduct risk, privacy, recordkeeping rules, legacy core systems, reputational sensitivity, regulated model validation processes.
- Likely beneficiaries: Firms with proprietary customer and transaction data, financial software providers embedded in workflows, scaled platforms that can automate service economics, exchanges and data providers selling enabling infrastructure.
- Incumbents at risk: Commodity service layers reliant on manual processing, smaller institutions lacking data and tech budgets, fee pools vulnerable to automated advice or simpler servicing models.
- Important KPIs or signals investors should watch: Efficiency ratio, headcount per account or advisor, fraud loss trends, claims cycle time, cross-sell rates, onboarding completion, assets per advisor, customer service cost to serve, digital adoption.
- Sector-specific analytical traps to avoid: Do not assume Gen AI can replace regulated judgment in credit, advice, or claims decisions; do not ignore model-risk governance costs; do not mistake chatbot deployment for meaningful unit-economics improvement.
