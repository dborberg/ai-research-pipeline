## Sector Adapter: Semiconductors

- Sector definition: Chip designers, foundries, equipment vendors, memory producers, electronic design automation providers, and semiconductor IP suppliers.
- Main business models: Unit sales, wafer starts, equipment revenue, recurring tool licenses, IP royalties, service and support.
- Value chain notes: Design, verification, EDA, fabrication, packaging, testing, and end-market demand all determine where Gen AI matters economically.
- Key revenue drivers: End-market unit demand, content per device, capex cycles, node transitions, memory pricing, fab utilization, design win momentum.
- Key cost centers: R&D, fabrication capex, equipment depreciation, manufacturing yields, packaging, inventory, sales and support.
- High-probability Gen AI use cases: EDA workflow assistance, verification productivity, design documentation, software stack optimization, customer support, sales engineering productivity, demand forecasting.
- Adoption bottlenecks: Extreme validation requirements, long design cycles, export controls, capital intensity, packaging constraints, concentration risk in manufacturing and hyperscaler demand.
- Likely beneficiaries: Compute suppliers exposed to AI infrastructure demand, memory and networking players leveraged to model training and inference, EDA vendors helping design complexity, equipment vendors tied to capacity build-out.
- Incumbents at risk: Segments dependent on mature cyclical demand with limited AI exposure, vendors facing mix shifts away from lower-value components, companies without clear participation in accelerated computing or enabling tools.
- Important KPIs or signals investors should watch: AI-related revenue mix, backlog quality, utilization, memory pricing, capex plans, packaging lead times, design win commentary, hyperscaler spend trends, gross margin by product mix.
- Sector-specific analytical traps to avoid: Do not reduce the sector to only GPU demand; do not overlook cyclicality and customer concentration; do not assume Gen AI demand offsets weakness in all legacy end markets.
