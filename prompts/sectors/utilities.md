## Sector Adapter: Utilities

- Sector definition: Regulated electric, gas, and water utilities, merchant power producers, grid operators, and related service providers.
- Main business models: Regulated returns on rate base, customer billing, generation margins in competitive markets, grid services, and infrastructure development.
- Value chain notes: Planning, generation, transmission, distribution, outage response, customer service, regulatory filings, and capital project execution are the main operating layers.
- Key revenue drivers: Rate-base growth, allowed returns, customer growth, load trends, generation mix, power prices in merchant exposure, infrastructure approvals.
- Key cost centers: Fuel where relevant, maintenance, labor, outage response, customer service, vegetation management, compliance, capex program execution.
- High-probability Gen AI use cases: Customer service automation, regulatory drafting support, field knowledge retrieval, outage communications, maintenance documentation, engineering and permitting workflow assistance.
- Adoption bottlenecks: Regulated cost recovery, mission-critical reliability requirements, cybersecurity sensitivity, long procurement cycles, legacy systems, limited tolerance for operational error.
- Likely beneficiaries: Utilities reducing service costs without compromising reliability, grid software vendors, engineering and field service providers improving documentation and planning productivity.
- Incumbents at risk: Manual service organizations, vendors reliant on documentation-heavy workflows, utilities slow to modernize customer and regulatory processes.
- Important KPIs or signals investors should watch: O&M efficiency, outage duration, call-center cost, regulatory approval pace, capex execution, customer satisfaction, workforce productivity, digital self-service adoption.
- Sector-specific analytical traps to avoid: Do not frame utilities as fast adopters of frontier AI in core operations; do not ignore that many gains may be shared with customers through regulation; do not confuse grid digitization broadly with Gen AI specifically.
