## Sector Adapter: Communication Services

- Sector definition: Telecom operators, media companies, digital advertising platforms, streaming providers, publishers, and communications infrastructure businesses.
- Main business models: Subscription revenue, advertising revenue, content licensing, carriage fees, usage fees, enterprise connectivity, platform monetization.
- Value chain notes: Content creation, audience acquisition, ad targeting, network operations, customer service, churn management, and distribution rights shape the sector.
- Key revenue drivers: Subscriber growth, churn, ARPU, ad load, pricing, engagement, content ROI, network quality, enterprise contract wins.
- Key cost centers: Content production and licensing, network capex and opex, customer acquisition, sales commissions, support, moderation, billing.
- High-probability Gen AI use cases: Customer service automation, ad creative generation, campaign optimization support, content tagging and localization, enterprise sales support, coding and network operations assistance.
- Adoption bottlenecks: Copyright and licensing issues, brand safety, regulatory scrutiny, reputational risk, compute cost for scaled consumer experiences, telecom legacy systems.
- Likely beneficiaries: Ad platforms with data and distribution, telecoms automating service economics, content businesses reducing production support costs, infrastructure and software vendors selling into communications workflows.
- Incumbents at risk: Customer-service-heavy telecom models, content intermediaries exposed to automated creation and discovery, media workflows reliant on repetitive manual production tasks.
- Important KPIs or signals investors should watch: Churn, support cost per subscriber, ad yield, content ROI, engagement quality, ARPU, call-center deflection, sales productivity, capex intensity.
- Sector-specific analytical traps to avoid: Do not conflate AI-generated content volume with monetizable audience demand; do not ignore copyright and platform liability; do not assume telecom economics change quickly without regulatory and pricing flexibility.
