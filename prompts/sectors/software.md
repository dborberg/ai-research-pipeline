## Sector Adapter: Software

- Sector definition: Application software, infrastructure software, developer tools, cybersecurity, workflow software, and SaaS platforms.
- Main business models: Subscription revenue, usage-based pricing, seat licenses, support and services, marketplace take rates, enterprise platform bundles.
- Value chain notes: Product development, go-to-market, implementation, customer support, renewals, ecosystem distribution, and platform integration all matter.
- Key revenue drivers: Net retention, seat expansion, usage growth, new logo wins, pricing power, attach rates, partner distribution, developer adoption.
- Key cost centers: R&D, sales and marketing, cloud hosting, support, customer success, channel incentives.
- High-probability Gen AI use cases: Copilots embedded in workflows, support automation, code generation and testing assist, content generation, search and knowledge tools, sales enablement, implementation acceleration.
- Adoption bottlenecks: Inference cost, model quality variance, willingness to pay, feature commoditization, security concerns, enterprise change management, proving durable ROI beyond novelty.
- Likely beneficiaries: Platforms with entrenched distribution, proprietary workflow data, natural integration points, and pricing power to monetize AI features; infrastructure vendors selling picks-and-shovels into software demand.
- Incumbents at risk: Single-feature SaaS with weak moats, software sold on UI convenience rather than system-of-record position, support-heavy models that may face lower pricing power as automation spreads.
- Important KPIs or signals investors should watch: Net revenue retention, AI attach rate, gross margin after inference cost, support cost per customer, seat expansion, developer productivity claims tied to release velocity, churn by customer cohort.
- Sector-specific analytical traps to avoid: Do not assume every AI feature justifies higher pricing; do not ignore margin pressure from compute costs; do not confuse user engagement with durable monetization or defensibility.
