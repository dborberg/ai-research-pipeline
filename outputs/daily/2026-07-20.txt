<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s strongest theme is that the AI cycle is increasingly being shaped by physical capacity, governance readiness, and production deployment rather than model capability alone. TSMC’s Arizona ramp, Bristol Myers Squibb’s dedicated AI factory, and rising data-center backlash all point to AI becoming a long-duration infrastructure buildout with semiconductor, power, permitting, and balance-sheet implications. At the same time, employer AI policies, China’s product restrictions, and Australia’s proposed duty-of-care rules show that governance is becoming a gatekeeper for enterprise adoption. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key message is that AI exposure is broadening beyond the largest platforms into semiconductors, data-center infrastructure, enterprise controls, compliance software, and workflow redesign.</p>
