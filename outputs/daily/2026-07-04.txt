<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s strongest signal is that the AI investment cycle is becoming a physical infrastructure, capital allocation, and governance story rather than just a model-performance story. Institutional capital is still moving into AI data centers, semiconductor companies are using digital manufacturing tools to accelerate fab execution, and local governments are increasingly testing the pace of data center expansion through moratoriums and permitting scrutiny. At the same time, policy direction is diverging, with lighter U.S. regulation discussed alongside new AI governance proposals in Asia. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key takeaway is to monitor second-derivative beneficiaries, including semiconductor capacity, data centers, power and cooling, industrial automation, and compliance infrastructure, while keeping valuation discipline as AI monetization and regulation evolve.</p>
