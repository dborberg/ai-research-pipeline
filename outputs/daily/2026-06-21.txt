<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s strongest signal is that the AI investment cycle is becoming less about model novelty and more about the physical, regulatory, and operational conditions required to scale AI in the real world. Local data-center moratoriums, nuclear-power stock sensitivity, defense automation, and AI coding productivity research all point to a market where execution bottlenecks matter as much as demand. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the useful framing is that AI exposure now extends beyond mega-cap platforms into power, permitting, real estate, industrial automation, governance, and workflow redesign. The portfolio implication is to monitor both first-order AI leaders and second-derivative beneficiaries or constraints that determine how quickly AI capacity and adoption can compound.</p>
