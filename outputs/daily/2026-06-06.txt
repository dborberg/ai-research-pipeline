<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s strongest signal is that the AI investment cycle is increasingly constrained by real-world infrastructure, permitting, power, and policy bottlenecks rather than model capability alone. State and local data center moratorium activity is rising just as large-scale AI capacity commitments continue to expand globally, creating a sharper divide between demand for compute and the ability to build it quickly. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key takeaway is that AI exposure is no longer just about software platforms and semiconductors; it also requires monitoring utilities, grid equipment, cooling, land use, policy risk, and financing capacity.</p>

<h3>WHAT TO WATCH</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>State and city data center moratorium decisions over the next 30 to 90 days:</strong> Watch whether proposed pauses become signed law, get narrowed through exemptions, or trigger project relocations, because the outcome will affect regional AI capacity, utility planning, and infrastructure-equipment demand.</li>
<li><strong>Power availability and interconnection timelines for large AI campuses:</strong> Track whether grid capacity becomes the binding constraint on new compute supply, because delays could shift value toward utilities, transmission equipment, backup power, cooling, and energy-management providers.</li>
<li><strong>Cloud platform releases that add governance, evaluation, permissions, and model choice:</strong> Monitor whether enterprises adopt AI through controlled production workflows rather than ad hoc chatbot use, because that would favor platforms embedded in developer, data, security, and productivity ecosystems.</li>
<li><strong>Commercial robotics deployments beyond demonstrations:</strong> Look for repeat orders, fleet expansion, service contracts, and customer case studies, because physical AI needs evidence of durable unit economics before becoming a broader public-market theme.</li>
<li><strong>Government involvement in strategic AI companies and infrastructure:</strong> Watch for proposals involving equity stakes, procurement preferences, export controls, or financing support, because policy could increasingly shape which AI business models scale and where capital flows.</li>
