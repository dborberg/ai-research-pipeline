<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s strongest signal is that the AI investment cycle is broadening from models and applications into power, permitting, sovereign policy, and frontier hardware. Data center growth is now colliding with electricity costs, local zoning, and national energy strategy, which makes AI infrastructure a political and physical bottleneck as much as a technology opportunity. At the same time, capital is still flowing into AI and robotics, suggesting investors are looking beyond the largest platforms toward compute suppliers, data center vendors, autonomous systems, and humanoid components. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key message is that AI exposure increasingly requires monitoring the entire enabling stack, not just software leaders.</p>
