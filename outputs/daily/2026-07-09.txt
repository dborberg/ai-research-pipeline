<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>The dominant signal today is that AI is becoming a physical infrastructure, permitting, and production-readiness story, not just a model-performance story. Reuters’ reporting on grid-equipment shortages, Meta’s chip and data center expansion, and multiple local data center moratoriums all point to capacity constraints moving from GPUs to power, land use, and community approval. At the same time, enterprise examples in healthcare, manufacturing, and agent frameworks show AI moving toward governed workflows with measurable cost and productivity metrics. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the implication is that AI exposure increasingly requires monitoring second-derivative beneficiaries and bottlenecks across power equipment, optical networking, governance software, data platforms, and industrial automation.</p>
