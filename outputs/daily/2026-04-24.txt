<li><strong>The YCUA Board approved a 12-month moratorium on providing water to data centers:</strong> Water access is emerging alongside power as a practical constraint on where AI infrastructure can scale. <li><strong>EE Times said the Strait of Hormuz disruption exposed semiconductor supply chain vulnerabilities:</strong> The article points to risks around helium, logistics routes, and concentrated upstream inputs that can tighten chip availability and raise costs. For advisors, it is a useful reminder that AI infrastructure exposure includes geopolitical and supply-chain fragility beyond the fabs themselves. (Source: EE Times)</li>
</ul>
