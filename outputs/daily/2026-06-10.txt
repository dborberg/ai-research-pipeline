<h2>Beyond the Horizon: Daily Riffs from the Gen AI Songbook</h2>
<p><strong>June 10, 2026</strong></p>

<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>The strongest signal today is that the AI investment cycle is broadening from model capability toward constrained, governed, and geographically contested deployment. Large platforms are still driving demand, but local data center moratoriums, national-security oversight, privacy-focused cloud inference, and domestic robotics funding show that physical capacity and regulatory access are becoming as important as software innovation. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key message is that AI exposure increasingly runs through infrastructure, power, permitting, cybersecurity, governance, and industrial automation, not only through the largest model developers.</p>

<h3>TOP STORIES</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Meta tied up with Reliance for an AI data center in India:</strong> The partnership points to hyperscalers using local strategic partners to secure compute capacity in large growth markets, reinforcing that AI scale depends on power, land, telecom connectivity, and jurisdiction-specific execution. The portfolio read-through extends beyond Meta to data center operators, electrical equipment, cooling, fiber, grid services, and regional telecom infrastructure. (Source: Reuters)</li>
<li><strong>Flint passed a one-year moratorium on data centers:</strong> The city’s pause shows how local permitting, resource concerns, and community politics can slow AI infrastructure expansion even when demand for compute remains high. Advisors can frame this as evidence that the AI buildout is becoming a real-assets and policy bottleneck story, not just a cloud spending story. (Source: Crain's Detroit)</li>
<li><strong>The White House reportedly reined in an AI-testing unit amid national-security concerns:</strong> The move suggests federal AI oversight may become more centralized or more sensitive where frontier systems intersect with security risks. The implication is that model developers, cloud providers, federal contractors, and governance vendors may face a more complex compliance environment as AI becomes strategically important infrastructure. (Source: WSJ)</li>
</ul>
