<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s AI news points to a widening investment cycle in which the bottleneck is no longer just model quality, but the capital, governance, infrastructure, and physical deployment layers needed to make AI useful at scale. Large financings in physical AI and governed risk operations show investors backing platforms that can move AI from experimentation into real-world workflows. At the same time, data center moratoriums and policy proposals show that compute growth is increasingly constrained by local permitting, public acceptance, power availability, and regulation. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key message is that AI exposure is broadening beyond mega-cap platforms into robotics, compliance software, edge semiconductors, industrial automation, and infrastructure enablers, while execution risk is rising around physical buildout and governance.</p>
