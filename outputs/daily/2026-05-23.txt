<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s strongest signal is that the AI investment cycle is broadening from models and chips into the harder-to-scale layers of infrastructure, policy, and embedded platforms. Data center siting, inference latency, vehicle compute, and government technology funding all point to AI becoming a physical and regulatory buildout, not just a software adoption story. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key takeaway is that AI exposure increasingly runs through second-derivative beneficiaries such as power, grid, data center services, automotive semiconductors, governance tools, and industrial automation. The portfolio discussion should balance enthusiasm for AI demand with discipline around permitting risk, capital intensity, valuation, and policy uncertainty.</p>
