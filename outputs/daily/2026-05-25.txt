<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s AI news points to a cycle that is increasingly constrained by physical infrastructure, local policy, and production-grade governance rather than model capability alone. Data center growth is creating demand for electrical equipment, power management, cooling, siting approvals, and energy-aware compute architectures, while local governments are starting to push back on land, water, emissions, and grid concerns. At the same time, enterprise AI is moving toward longer workflow automation, but adoption depends on authentication, permissions, auditability, and compliance. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key message is that AI exposure is broadening beyond the largest model and cloud platforms into infrastructure suppliers, governance layers, industrial automation, and policy-risk monitoring.</p>
