<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s strongest signal is that the AI cycle is increasingly being shaped by physical capacity, policy access, and infrastructure economics rather than model launches alone. Chip supply coordination, hyperscale cloud investment, local data center scrutiny, and company-level semiconductor targets all point to AI becoming a capital-intensive buildout with regulatory and resource bottlenecks. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key framing is that AI exposure is broadening beyond the largest platforms into semiconductors, data centers, power, cooling, water stewardship, compliance, and industrial automation. The investment discussion should focus on whether usage, pricing, financing, and permitting can keep pace with the scale of planned AI infrastructure spending.</p>
