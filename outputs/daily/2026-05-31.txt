<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s strongest signal is that the AI cycle is becoming a physical infrastructure, permitting, and capital-allocation story as much as a software story. Large data center plans, local moratorium fights, and community opposition show that compute growth increasingly depends on power, land use, interconnection, and political acceptance. At the same time, industrial and enterprise examples suggest AI is moving into production workflows where speed, coordination, and auditability matter. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key framing is that AI beneficiaries may broaden from model providers and mega-cap platforms toward infrastructure, industrial automation, governance, and workflow-enablement layers.</p>
