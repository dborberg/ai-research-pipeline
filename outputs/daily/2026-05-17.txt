<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>The dominant signal today is that the AI investment cycle is becoming a permitting, power, and governance cycle, not just a model-performance race. Multiple local actions against data center expansion show that physical constraints and community acceptance can affect the timing and cost of AI capacity buildouts. At the same time, policy proposals and boardroom governance pressure suggest enterprises will need more formal controls before AI can scale broadly. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key takeaway is to monitor both the direct AI leaders and the second-derivative enablers tied to power, compliance, infrastructure, and implementation risk.</p>
