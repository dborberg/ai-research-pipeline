<p><strong>July 15, 2026</strong></p>

<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s strongest signal is that AI is becoming a governed infrastructure cycle, not just a model or software cycle. Australia’s national AI policy push, U.S. state-level data center scrutiny, and the BIS warning on debt-funded AI investment all point to a market where power, water, permitting, financing, and governance can shape adoption as much as model quality. At the same time, enterprise examples in healthcare compliance, telecom networks, agent workflows, and robotics show AI moving toward production systems with auditability, orchestration, and real-world deployment requirements. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key message is that AI exposure should be monitored across infrastructure bottlenecks, financing discipline, enterprise workflow platforms, compliance layers, and physical AI rather than only the largest model providers.</p>
