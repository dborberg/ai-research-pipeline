<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>Today’s AI news points to an investment cycle that is broadening from model excitement into production deployment, physical infrastructure, and governance friction. Large enterprises and government agencies are using AI in higher-value workflows, while chip, robotics, and data-center developments show that the enabling stack remains capital intensive. At the same time, local opposition, labor policy, and federal rulemaking uncertainty are becoming real constraints on how quickly AI infrastructure and automation can scale. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key message is that AI exposure is no longer just about the largest platforms; the next phase increasingly involves power, permitting, industrial automation, compliance, workflow software, and capital discipline.</p>
