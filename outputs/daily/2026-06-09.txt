<h3>TOP THEME OF THE DAY</h3>
<p>The AI investment cycle is increasingly becoming a physical buildout, policy, and capital-allocation story rather than only a model-performance story. Today’s news shows governments, hyperscalers, local permitting bodies, labor markets, and infrastructure providers all shaping the pace of AI deployment. For CPM, advisor, and wholesaler conversations, the key point is that AI adoption depends on real-world bottlenecks: power, construction labor, cooling, data center siting, national industrial policy, and governance readiness. The second-derivative opportunity set continues to broaden beyond the largest platforms into utilities, power equipment, construction trades, cooling, semiconductors, compliance, defense autonomy, and robotics infrastructure.</p>
