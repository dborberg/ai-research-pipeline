AI PRACTICE TIP OF THE WEEK
What: Use AI to build a one-page “client-specific risk discussion map” before a review meeting by turning that household’s holdings, concentration points, and recent market themes into 5 to 7 tailored talking points and questions.

Why: Advisors often know the portfolio well but still spend too much time deciding which risks are most worth discussing with each client. This helps you quickly surface where a client may be overexposed to a theme like AI infrastructure, mega-cap concentration, or industrial automation and turns that into a more thoughtful conversation instead of a generic review. It is also a simple way to connect current headlines to the client’s actual portfolio without sounding reactive.

How to:
Paste in the client’s top holdings, sector weights, and any known concentration concerns.
Ask AI to identify 3 portfolio-specific risks, 2 opportunity areas, and 5 plain-English questions to discuss in the meeting.
Review the output, remove anything speculative, and use it as your internal prep sheet.

Copy prompt: You are helping me prepare for a client portfolio review. Based on the holdings and weights below, create a one-page meeting prep sheet with: 1) the top 3 portfolio-specific risks worth discussing, 2) 2 potential opportunity areas or diversification ideas, 3) any concentration issues by stock, sector, or theme, and 4) 5 plain-English questions I can ask the client to explore risk tolerance, time horizon, and interest in rebalancing. Keep it practical, balanced, and client-friendly. Do not write performance claims or recommendations. Here are the holdings and notes: [paste holdings, weights, sector exposure, and client notes].

Guardrail: Do not paste full account numbers, personal identifiers, or nonpublic client information. Treat the output as meeting prep only and verify any portfolio observations, tax implications, or recommendations before using them with a client.
