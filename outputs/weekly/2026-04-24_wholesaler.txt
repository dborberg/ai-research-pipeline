Beyond the Horizon: Weekly Riffs from the Gen AI Songbook
Week Ending April 24, 2026

TOP 5 STORIES THIS WEEK

1. Applied Digital signed a $7.5 billion AI data center lease with a U.S. hyperscaler, one of the clearest long-term infrastructure commitments tied to AI demand this week. For advisors, it is a concrete signal that AI spending is still flowing into physical capacity, which keeps data centers, power access, and infrastructure suppliers central to the conversation beyond software alone. (Reuters)

2. Microsoft said it will invest $18 billion to expand cloud and AI capacity in Australia, extending the hyperscaler buildout into another major developed market. For advisors, that matters because it reinforces that AI capex is still global and infrastructure-heavy, with implications for utilities, construction, networking, and data center ecosystems. (Data Center Dynamics)

3. Amazon expanded its strategic collaboration with Anthropic, deepening ties around AI development and computing capacity through AWS. For advisors, this is a timely reminder that cloud platforms and model developers are locking in closer infrastructure relationships, which can shape where enterprise AI workloads and related spending concentrate. (About Amazon)

4. Neura Robotics and AWS announced a strategic collaboration to scale physical AI, including integration with Amazon SageMaker and potential deployment of Neura robots in select Amazon fulfillment centers. For advisors, this matters because it ties AI directly to warehouse automation and shows robotics moving from concept to operational testing inside a named enterprise environment. (Robotics & Automation News)

5. Reliable Robotics raised $160 million to accelerate deployment of its FAA-certifiable autonomous aircraft system and scale production, while citing more than 200 commercial and military commitments. For advisors, that is a real-world sign that AI capital is reaching regulated, safety-critical industries where certification and government demand can matter as much as software adoption. (The Robot Report)

BEYOND THE MAG 7

1. Applied Digital stood out this week because its $7.5 billion lease shows AI demand is creating real value for specialized data center operators, not just the cloud giants leasing the capacity. That gives advisors a fact-based way to discuss second-order beneficiaries tied to powered land, colocation, and infrastructure execution. (Reuters)

2. Flex and Teradyne Robotics expanded their partnership so Flex will both use Teradyne robots in its own factories and manufacture key robotics components. That matters now because it shows industrial automation demand is spreading through manufacturing supply chains, not just through standalone robot vendors. (Robotics & Automation News)

3. Oklo partnered with Nvidia and Los Alamos on AI-driven advanced nuclear infrastructure development. For advisors, this is a timely example of AI demand pulling adjacent sectors like power generation and energy infrastructure into the opportunity set. (Data Center Dynamics)
