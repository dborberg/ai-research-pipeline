AI PRACTICE TIP OF THE WEEK
What: Use AI to create a “referral handoff prep card” before the first call with a referred prospect.

Why: Referrals often come with just enough context to be useful, but not enough to assume needs or pitch too quickly. A short prep card helps the advisor honor the relationship, open with relevance, and ask better first-meeting questions. This week’s AI infrastructure headlines are a good reminder that many clients are hearing big themes but need help connecting them to practical planning issues.

How to:
1. Paste in the referral source, what they said about the person, and the meeting format.
2. Ask AI for a warm opening, likely priorities, and 5 discovery questions.
3. Review for accuracy and remove anything that sounds assumptive or too sales-focused.

Copy prompt: Create a referral handoff prep card for my first meeting with a referred prospect. Context: [paste only non-sensitive referral notes]. Referrer relationship: [client/COI/friend]. Meeting format: [phone/video/in person]. Include: 1) a respectful opening that acknowledges the referral without oversharing, 2) what I should not assume, 3) 5 discovery questions that connect their likely situation to planning needs, 4) one timely market or AI-related topic I could mention only if relevant, and 5) a simple objective for the meeting. Keep it concise and advisor-friendly.

Guardrail: Do not include private client details, account numbers, or confidential information in the prompt. Treat AI’s output as prep only; verify facts and keep the actual conversation suitability- and compliance-aligned.
